# Objection Handling Playbook

## The LAER Framework

For every objection, follow this sequence:

1. **Listen** — Let the prospect fully express their concern
2. **Acknowledge** — Validate their concern ("That's a fair point...")
3. **Explore** — Ask clarifying questions to understand the real issue
4. **Respond** — Address the specific concern with evidence

## Common Objections by Category

### Price Objections

**"It's too expensive"**
- Ask: "Compared to what?" (Understand their reference point)
- Reframe as ROI: "Our customers typically see X return within Y months"
- Break down the cost: "That's less than $X per day/per user"
- Total cost of ownership: "What's the current cost of NOT solving this problem?"

**"We don't have the budget right now"**
- Ask: "When does your budget cycle reset?"
- Offer: Phased implementation, monthly billing, pilot program
- Quantify cost of delay: "Every month without this costs you approximately $X"

**"Your competitor is cheaper"**
- Ask: "What's included in their pricing?"
- Highlight hidden costs: implementation, support, training
- Feature comparison: "They don't include X, Y, Z which you mentioned needing"

### Timing Objections

**"We're not ready yet"**
- Ask: "What would need to happen for you to be ready?"
- Offer: "What if we started with a small pilot that doesn't require full commitment?"
- Plant urgency: "Many companies in your industry are already implementing this"

**"Call me back in 6 months"**
- Ask: "What changes in 6 months?" (Understand the real blocker)
- Offer: "Can I send you some materials to review in the meantime?"
- Set specific follow-up: "I'll reach out on [specific date]. Does that work?"

### Authority Objections

**"I need to talk to my boss/team"**
- Ask: "What would be most helpful for that conversation?"
- Offer: "I can put together a one-pager with the key points and ROI data"
- Suggest: "Would it be helpful if I joined that meeting to answer technical questions?"

**"We have a committee that decides"**
- Ask: "Who's on the committee and what do they care most about?"
- Offer: "I can prepare tailored materials for each stakeholder"
- Ask: "When does the committee meet next?"

### Trust/Risk Objections

**"We've been burned by similar products before"**
- Ask: "What went wrong with the previous solution?"
- Show: Case studies from similar companies
- Offer: "We offer a 30-day trial / money-back guarantee"
- Provide: References from current customers in their industry

**"How do I know this will work for us?"**
- Offer: Pilot program with defined success metrics
- Share: Industry-specific case studies and testimonials
- Suggest: "Let's define what success looks like for you, and if we don't hit that, you can walk away"

### Status Quo Objections

**"We're fine with what we have"**
- Ask: "How long have you been using that approach?"
- Explore: "What would it look like if you could do X 40% faster?"
- Challenge gently: "Most companies I talk to didn't realize they had a problem until they saw what was possible"

**"We built our own solution"**
- Ask: "How much time does your team spend maintaining it?"
- Calculate: "What's the opportunity cost of your engineers maintaining this vs. building new features?"
- Acknowledge: "That shows great initiative. Our platform can integrate with what you've built"

## Business-Specific Objection Responses

### Automotive Intelligence
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Our technicians won't use AI" | "We designed it to augment, not replace. Techs see it as a power tool, not a replacement. 94% adoption rate in first 30 days." |
| "We already have diagnostic equipment" | "We integrate with your existing tools. Our AI layer adds predictive insights on top of what you already have." |
| "Too much training required" | "Average onboarding time is 2 hours per technician. We provide on-site training included in the package." |
