# Sales Conversation Stages Reference

## Stage Definitions

### Stage 1: Introduction
- Introduce yourself and your company
- Be polite and respectful, keep tone professional
- Greeting should be welcoming
- Always clarify why you are calling/reaching out
- **Do NOT pitch in your first turn** — just greet and ask how they're doing

### Stage 2: Qualification
- Confirm prospect is the right person to talk to
- Verify they have authority to make purchasing decisions
- Ask about their role, responsibilities, and decision-making process
- If they're not the right person, ask for a warm introduction

### Stage 3: Value Proposition
- Briefly explain how your product/service benefits the prospect
- Focus on unique selling points vs. competitors
- Keep it concise — this is a teaser, not a full presentation
- Tailor the value prop to what you've learned so far

### Stage 4: Needs Analysis
- Ask open-ended questions to uncover needs and pain points
- Listen carefully and take notes
- Key questions:
  - "What's your biggest challenge with X right now?"
  - "How are you currently handling Y?"
  - "What would an ideal solution look like for you?"
  - "What's the impact of this problem on your business?"
- Let the prospect talk more than you do

### Stage 5: Solution Presentation
- Present your product/service as the solution to their specific pain points
- Map features directly to the needs they expressed
- Use their own language back to them
- Provide specific numbers, case studies, or ROI data when available

### Stage 6: Objection Handling
- Address objections with evidence, testimonials, and data
- Common objections and frameworks:
  - **Price**: ROI calculation, total cost of ownership, payment plans
  - **Timing**: Cost of delay, quick implementation, phased rollout
  - **Competition**: Feature comparison, unique differentiators, case studies
  - **Risk**: Free trial, money-back guarantee, pilot program
  - **Authority**: Provide materials for internal stakeholder presentation
- Never dismiss an objection — acknowledge it first, then address it

### Stage 7: Close
- Propose a clear next step:
  - Demo booking
  - Free trial signup
  - Meeting with decision-makers
  - Pilot program
  - Contract review
- Summarize what was discussed
- Reiterate the key benefits
- Create gentle urgency without pressure
- Offer two options (not one): "Would Tuesday or Thursday work better?"

### Stage 8: End Conversation
- Triggers:
  - Prospect needs to leave
  - Prospect is not interested
  - Next steps have been determined
  - Maximum turns reached
- Always end gracefully and leave the door open
- Confirm any commitments made

## Stage Transition Signals

| From → To | Prospect Signals |
|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 → 2 | Responds to greeting, engages in conversation |
| 2 → 3 | Confirms they're the right person |
| 3 → 4 | Shows interest in value proposition |
| 4 → 5 | Shares specific pain points and needs |
| 5 → 6 | Raises concerns or objections |
| 6 → 7 | Objections resolved, shows buying signals |
| 7 → 8 | Agrees to next step or declines |
| Any → 8 | "Not interested", "Have to go", "Call back later" |
| Any → 4 | Asks new questions about their situation |
| Any → 6 | Raises new objections |

## Conversation Metrics to Track

- **Stage velocity**: How quickly conversations progress through stages
- **Drop-off stage**: Which stage prospects most commonly disengage
- **Objection frequency**: Most common objections by stage
- **Close rate by entry channel**: call vs. email vs. chat vs. linkedin
- **Average turns to close**: Conversations turns needed to reach Stage 7
